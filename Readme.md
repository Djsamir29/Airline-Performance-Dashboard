# Airline Performance Analyze Dashboard (Power BI)

# Project Overview

This project analyzes airline operational performance using an interactive Power BI dashboard built on an Airline Performance dataset. The goal of the analysis is to monitor key business metrics such as revenue, profit, passenger load, route performance, and flight delays.

The dashboard helps identify profitable routes, analyze delay patterns across regions, and understand the relationship between revenue and profit. These insights can support airline management in improving operational efficiency and optimizing route strategies.

The dashboard enables stakeholders to quickly explore trends in airline operations and make data-driven decisions.

# Dataset
Airline Performance.Xlsx


# KPIs in the Dashboard

The dashboard highlights the following key performance indicators:

Total Revenue – Overall revenue generated by airline routes.

Total Profit – Net profit after operational costs.

Average Delay (Minutes) – Average delay experienced by flights.

Average Passenger Load – Average number of passengers per flight.

Most Profitable Route – Route generating the highest profit.

# Key Insights from the Analysis

1️⃣ Revenue Performance

Total revenue generated is approximately $12M across routes.

Daily revenue trends show strong fluctuations, indicating demand variability across dates.

2️⃣ Route Profitability

DFW–SEA and SFO–TYO are among the most profitable routes.

These routes contribute the highest share of revenue in the dataset.

3️⃣ Passenger Load

Average passenger load is around 190 passengers per flight, indicating relatively high seat utilization.

4️⃣ Flight Delays

International flights experience higher delays (~96 minutes) compared to domestic flights (~83 minutes).

This may be due to longer travel distances, airport congestion, or customs processes.

5️⃣ Revenue vs Profit Relationship

The scatter plot shows a positive correlation between revenue and profit, meaning higher revenue routes tend to generate higher profits.

# Business Recommendations

1️⃣ Focus on High-Performing Routes
Routes such as DFW–SEA and SFO–TYO generate the highest revenue and profit. Increasing flight frequency on these routes could increase profitability.

2️⃣ Reduce International Flight Delays
International routes have significantly higher delays. Improving scheduling efficiency and airport coordination may reduce delays.

3️⃣ Monitor Low Performing Routes
Routes with lower revenue contribution should be evaluated for pricing optimization or operational cost adjustments.

4️⃣ Use Demand-Based Scheduling
Revenue fluctuations indicate seasonal or daily demand patterns. Airlines can optimize flight scheduling based on demand trends.

# Conclusion

This project demonstrates how data visualization and analytics can support airline operational decisions. The dashboard provides clear insights into revenue trends, route profitability, and delay patterns, helping stakeholders identify opportunities for improving efficiency and maximizing profit.
